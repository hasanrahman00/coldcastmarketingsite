What I'd look for

Option B is the one to judge hardest. Scroll it to the dashboard, where a lime button and a mint chart sit in one viewport. Two greens ~94° apart tend to read as an accident rather than a decision — that's the specific thing to check with your eyes, because if it looks deliberate to you, my objection is wrong.
The trade-off in one line: mint quietly says verified; lime says electric — and, on a product selling “0 bans, safest”, possibly caution. Lime is also genuinely hotter (about 15.8:1 on graphite vs mint's 11:1), so it's superb on small accents and type but can fatigue as a large fill. Note the button ink has to change too: #062119 is a dark green and goes muddy on lime, so these use #131a00.
